About

There's no founder photo on this page. That's deliberate.

Span Protocol isn't a personal brand. It's an editorial property built on a simple bet: that longevity research deserves the same rigour as financial journalism, without needing a face attached to make it credible.

Why faceless

The research should be the thing you trust. Not a personality.

Most longevity content online is attached to a doctor, a self-experimenter, or a podcast host — which means the credibility of the information is tied to whether you trust that specific person. We wanted to try something else: let the citation do the work the personal brand usually does.

That means every post names its source. It means we publish corrections when the evidence shifts. And it means our growth depends entirely on whether the work holds up — not on parasocial connection to whoever is behind the account.

5× a week, every week, regardless of who's writing. The format doesn't depend on a single person's schedule.
How we work

Five principles behind every post.

01

Every claim traces to a source

If we can't cite it — a peer-reviewed study, a clinical trial, a named dataset — it doesn't go in the carousel. Mechanism-only claims are labelled as such.

02

We show the limitations, not just the finding

Cohort size, study design, demographic specificity — the caveats that make a finding trustworthy are part of the post, not an afterthought.

03

Protocols come with a minimum effective dose

We're not interested in maximalist routines. Every protocol stack states the smallest version of itself that the evidence actually supports.

04

Contrarian angles require contrarian evidence

We'll challenge a popular belief, but only when the data backs the challenge. A take has to survive being checked before it gets published.

05

Sponsored content follows the same rules

A paid placement doesn't get a lower evidence bar. If a claim wouldn't pass in an organic post, it doesn't pass in a sponsored one either.
